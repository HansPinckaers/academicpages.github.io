My research

My PhD, so far, has been about training a network memory efficient, resulting in a novel method which gives the possibility to train a ResNet34 with gigantic images (up to 268 megapixels) end-to-end with a single label per image.

  • Training convolutional neural networks with megapixel images
    • First proof of concept of my method to train memory efficiently.
    • Published at the MIDL 2018 conference.
  • Streaming convolutional neural networks for end-to-end learning with multi-megapixel images
    • Full paper about the method.
    • Read this if you want to know how it works.
    • Published in IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence. Read older preprint at arXiv
  • High resolution whole prostate biopsy classification using streaming stochastic gradient descent
    • First real application of the method.
    • Published at SPIE Medical Imaging 2019.
  • Detection of prostate cancer in whole-slide images through end-to-end training with image-level labels
    • Full paper about the application on prostate biopsies
